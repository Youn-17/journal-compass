## 1. Aims & Scope (VERBATIM)

Source: <https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education/about/aims-and-scope> (also mirrored verbatim in the Guide for Authors "Aims and scope" block). **Confidence: HIGH.**

> *Computers & Education* aims to increase knowledge and understanding of ways in which digital technology can enhance education, through the publication of high-quality research, which extends theory and practice. The Editors welcome research papers on the pedagogical uses of digital technology, where the focus is broad enough to be of interest to a wider education community.
>
> We do not publish small-scale evaluations of specific software/systems in specialist domains or particular courses in individual institutions (unless the findings have broader relevance that is explicitly drawn out in the paper). Papers that include discussions of the implementation of software and/or hardware should focus on the context of use, the user/system interface, usability issues and evaluations of the user experience and impacts on and particularly on the implications for learning and teaching. Computers as a delivery platform only is insufficient. Detailed information on implementation architecture should NOT be included in the paper, but may be provided via URLs.
>
> We welcome systematic review papers and meta-analyses that include clear research questions, a framework of analysis, and conclusions that reflect the aims of the paper. See PRISMA guidelines for further advice.
>
> Authors should take care to refer to and abide by the author guidelines. Papers that do not address the criteria outlined in the author guidelines will be returned without review.
>
> Authors are also welcome to submit to the journal's open access companion titles, Computers & Education Open or Computers & Education: Artificial Intelligence.

## 2. Guide for Authors — article types, limits, structure

Source: <https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education/publish/guide-for-authors> **Confidence: HIGH** (fetched verbatim).

### Article types accepted
- **Research papers** (empirical) — the default.
- **Systematic reviews and meta-analyses** — explicitly welcomed; must include clear research questions, a framework of analysis, conclusions reflecting the aims, and follow **PRISMA**.
- **Position papers** — NOT listed as accepted in C&E itself. (Note: position papers are explicitly welcomed only in the *AI* sister journal, not in the flagship. See §5.)

### Hard limits (VERBATIM)
> Articles should be no more than **8,000 words, excluding references and any appendixes.**

> You are required to provide a concise and factual abstract which does not exceed **250 words.**

- **Keywords:** "1 to 7 keywords"; avoid multi-word keywords using "and"/"of".
- **Highlights:** required; "3 to 5 bullet points, each a maximum of 85 characters, including spaces."
- **Graphical abstract:** *encouraged* (not required).
- **Referencing:** APA 7th edition (2020).

### Required structure (VERBATIM excerpts)
> Divide your article into clearly defined and numbered sections. Number subsections 1.1 (then 1.1.1, 1.1.2, …), then 1.2, etc.
> Do not include the article abstract within section numbering.

- **CRediT** author contribution statement required from corresponding author.
- Title page separate from anonymized manuscript (double-anonymized review).

**Inference (Confidence: MED):** The Guide imposes *no* mandated IMRaD section template beyond numbered sections, but combined with the Aims & Scope it implies the expected shape: framing in education theory/literature → RQs → method → results → implications for learning and teaching. The 8,000-word cap signals a focused empirical/review paper, not a sprawling systems paper.

## 3. Stated requirements: theory, outcomes, what they do NOT want

### Theoretical grounding — what they SAY
- Aims & Scope: research must "extend **theory** and practice." **Confidence: HIGH.** This is the closest thing to an explicit theory requirement — the journal frames itself as theory-extending, not merely applied.
- Systematic reviews must have "a **framework of analysis**." **HIGH.**

**Inference (MED):** "extends theory and practice" + "broad enough to be of interest to a wider education community" together function as a theoretical-contribution gate, but the Guide does not spell out a mandatory theory section.

### Educational / learning outcomes — what they SAY
- "impacts on and particularly on the **implications for learning and teaching**." **HIGH.** The pedagogical/learning payoff is the required focus, not the technology.
- "Computers as a delivery platform only is insufficient." **HIGH.**

### What they explicitly do NOT want (VERBATIM-backed, Confidence: HIGH)
1. **Small-scale evaluations** of specific software/systems in specialist domains. (unless broader relevance explicitly drawn out)
2. **Single-course / single-institution** studies without broader relevance.
3. **Tech-as-delivery-platform-only** papers ("insufficient").
4. **Implementation/architecture detail** in the body of the paper (push to URLs instead).
5. Papers that **don't meet the author-guideline criteria** → returned without review (desk reject).
6. **Generative-AI-created/altered images** in manuscripts (not permitted except where AI imaging is itself the research method, fully disclosed). Graphical abstracts may NOT use generative AI.

## 4. Ethics / data / AI-use policies they require (Confidence: HIGH, verbatim-sourced)

- **Research data:** Option C applies — authors are **required** to deposit data in a repository and cite/link it, OR provide a statement explaining why data cannot be shared. A **data statement is required** at submission.
- **Declaration of generative AI use:** authors **must** declare GenAI use in manuscript prep; AI Tools "must never be used as a substitute for human critical thinking"; AI cannot be listed as author. Reviewers/editors are NOT currently permitted to use GenAI in peer review.
- **Double-anonymized peer review**; typically ≥2 reviewers; editor makes final decision.
- **CRediT**, competing-interests declaration, funding disclosure all required.

## 5. The companion-journal split: what C&E KEEPS vs SPINS OFF

This is a family of journals, not a single split event. C&E (flagship, subscription + OA support) sits alongside three companions.

### What the flagship says about the companions (VERBATIM, HIGH)
Aims & Scope closes with:
> Authors are also welcome to submit to the journal's open access companion titles, **Computers & Education Open** or **Computers & Education: Artificial Intelligence.**

(Note: the flagship's own scope text names C&E Open and C&E:AI as companions but does not name C&E: X Reality in that closing line as fetched — the X Reality title exists as a separate sister journal, §5c.)

### 5a. Computers & Education: Artificial Intelligence (CAEAI) — ISSN 2666-920X
Source: <https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education-artificial-intelligence/about/aims-and-scope> **Confidence: HIGH (verbatim).** CiteScore shown: 28.7. Open access.

> *Computers & Education: Artificial Intelligence* aims at affording a world-wide platform for researchers, developers, and educators to present their research studies, exchange new ideas, and demonstrate novel systems and pedagogical innovations on the research topics in relation to applications of artificial intelligence (AI) in education and AI education. A sister journal to Computers & Education, CAEAI is an open access and peer reviewed journal, which welcomes **empirical, review, and position papers** from the following areas.

Topic list (verbatim): AI-assisted tutoring for decision-making; AI-based multimedia data analytics in classroom/school settings; AI-enabled learning environment; designing educational technologies in the age of AI; ethical issues raised by AI applications in education; applying AI techniques/models to analyze educational data; developing AI-based e-learning systems; innovative strategies/systems for AI education; intelligent tutoring and learning systems; learning behavioral and cognitive analysis for intelligent systems; learning emotion detection using AI; learning emotion and classroom dialogue analysis using AI; personalized and intelligent educational systems; pedagogical innovations for AI teaching and learning; research trends in AI for education research.

**Key division-of-labor signals:**
- CAEAI explicitly welcomes **"demonstrate novel systems"** and **position papers** — exactly the system-demonstration / architecture-heavy / position-paper work the FLAGSHIP excludes.
- So C&E offloads AI-system-building and AI-technique papers to CAEAI, and KEEPS the broad-education, theory-extending, learning-and-teaching-implication papers (even AI-themed ones, if written as education research first).

### 5b. Computers & Education Open (CAEO) — ISSN 2666-5573 (Confidence: MED)
Named as the OA companion. Functions as the open-access sibling for in-scope-but-overflow / OA-preferred education-technology research. (Aims/scope not fetched verbatim this pass; named verbatim by the flagship.)

### 5c. Computers & Education: X Reality (CEXR) — ISSN 2949-6780
Source: <https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education-x-reality/about/aims-and-scope> **Confidence: HIGH (verbatim).**

> *Computers and Education: X Reality* (CEXR) affords a platform for researchers, developers, and educators to publish their research progress, share novel research ideas, and demonstrate pedagogical innovation on topics relating to **mixed reality for educational purposes.** In particular, CEXR is interested in immersive learning, personalized and adaptive learning, smart education, and interactive learning.

Scope includes VR/AR/MR in e-learning, collaborative/multiuser VR/AR/MR, 3D interaction, immersive gaming/edutainment, metaverse, blockchain/NFTs in metaverse education, haptics, hardware/devices, ethical issues in VR/AR/MR, etc.

**Division-of-labor signal:** VR/AR/MR/metaverse/immersive-tech work — including hardware and device papers — is routed to CEXR. The flagship keeps the broader-interest pedagogy.

### Synthesis of the split (Inference, Confidence: MED)
What C&E **KEEPS**: broad-interest, theory-extending empirical and review research on pedagogical uses of digital technology, where learning/teaching implications dominate.
What it **SPINS OFF**:
- AI-as-technique, AI-system-building, AI position papers → **CAEAI**.
- VR/AR/MR/metaverse/immersive + related hardware → **CEXR**.
- OA-preferred / overflow education-technology research → **CAEO**.
The flagship's "no system-architecture detail," "no system demos," "computers-as-delivery-only insufficient" rules are the mirror image of what the companions explicitly invite.
