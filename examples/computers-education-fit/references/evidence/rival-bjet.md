# Contrast Analysis: Computers & Education (C&E) vs. British Journal of Educational Technology (BJET)

**Purpose:** Identify what genuinely DISTINGUISHES C&E from BJET so a writer knows which of these two sibling edtech journals to submit to. Field-wide norms shared by both (peer review, edtech topical breadth, anti-plagiarism, ethics) are deliberately excluded — only differences that pass a "Redirect Test" (would actually change which journal you send the paper to) are recorded.

**The two journals:**
- **C&E** — Elsevier, ISSN 0360-1315, EIC Chin-Chung Tsai. JIF ~11+ range. Article types: Articles, Reviews.
- **BJET** — Wiley on behalf of BERA (British Educational Research Association), ISSN 0007-1013. JIF ~8.1, CiteScore 17.6, ~4% acceptance rate, 26-day median to first decision.

**Confidence tags:** HIGH = direct quote from official journal page; MEDIUM = official-adjacent / consistently corroborated secondary source; LOW = inference from patterns, flagged as such.

---

## 0. Verbatim scope statements (anchor for everything below)

**C&E (official, verbatim):**
> "Computers & Education aims to increase knowledge and understanding of ways in which digital technology can enhance education, through the publication of high-quality research, which extends theory and practice. The Editors welcome research papers on the pedagogical uses of digital technology, where the focus is broad enough to be of interest to a wider education community. We do not publish small-scale evaluations of specific software/systems in specialist domains or particular courses in individual institutions (unless the findings have broader relevance that is explicitly drawn out in the paper). Papers that include discussions of the implementation of software and/or hardware should focus on the context of use, the user/system interface, usability issues and evaluations of the user experience and impacts on and particularly on the implications for learning and teaching. Computers as a delivery platform only is insufficient. Detailed information on implementation architecture should NOT be included in the paper... We welcome systematic review papers and meta-analyses that include clear research questions, a framework of analysis, and conclusions..."
- Source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education/about/aims-and-scope — Confidence: HIGH

**BJET (official, verbatim):**
> "We publish theoretical perspectives, methodological developments, and high-quality empirical research demonstrating whether and how applications of instructional/educational technology systems, networks, tools and resources lead to improvements in formal and non-formal education at all levels."
- Source: https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14678535 — Confidence: HIGH

The single most important structural difference is visible right here: **C&E's scope sentence names only "research papers" and "review papers/meta-analyses." BJET's scope sentence explicitly opens with "theoretical perspectives, methodological developments" BEFORE "empirical research."** This is the root of most contrasts below.

## 1. Scope & topic emphasis — what each favors

**DIFFERENCE 1a — Contribution menu.** BJET formally welcomes three distinct contribution types as co-equal headliners: (i) theoretical perspectives, (ii) methodological developments, (iii) empirical research. C&E's official scope advertises essentially two: empirical research papers and systematic reviews/meta-analyses. A pure-theory or pure-methodology paper has a named home in BJET's scope statement; it has no equivalent named slot in C&E's.
- Sources: https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14678535 ; https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education/about/aims-and-scope — Confidence: HIGH

**DIFFERENCE 1b — Short-form / work-in-progress / position pieces.** BJET runs a **Colloquium section** for "shorter contributions, summarising work in progress, raising queries and questioning received wisdom." C&E has no comparable short-form/position-piece vehicle; its article types are Articles and Reviews only.
- Sources: https://scispace.com/formats/wiley/british-journal-of-educational-technology/b3004212c42534d3a3af6e47dd99bb31 (Colloquium description) ; https://manusights.com/blog/computers-and-education-submission-guide (C&E article types = Articles, Reviews) — Confidence: MEDIUM (Colloquium long-standing; secondary corroboration)

**DIFFERENCE 1c — "Negative results" / failed initiatives.** BJET explicitly invites "reports on educational technology initiatives which met with problems and/or failed to achieve their aims, and the lessons to be learned." C&E's scope contains no such invitation; its filter is the opposite — it screens OUT narrow evaluations unless broad relevance is explicit. A "what went wrong with our edtech rollout" paper is on-brand for BJET, off-brand for C&E.
- Source for BJET: search-surfaced BJET scope text, https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/hub/journal/14678535/forauthors.html — Confidence: MEDIUM (consistently quoted from BJET guidelines)

**DIFFERENCE 1d — Implementation / practice / system-level studies.** BJET leans toward implementation, multi-site, longitudinal and system/institution-level studies (it ran a special section on "EdTech implementation research in LMICs" and welcomes "multi-site, multi-perspective studies... longitudinal studies that evidence the benefits of educational technology innovations to systems and institutions"). C&E by contrast is wary of any single-setting study and demands that implementation detail be subordinated to learning/teaching implications ("Computers as a delivery platform only is insufficient. Detailed information on implementation architecture should NOT be included").
- Sources: https://edtechhub.org/2025/01/10/call-for-papers-the-british-journal-of-education-technology/ ; https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education/about/aims-and-scope — Confidence: HIGH

**SHARED (NOT a discriminator):** Both cover AI in education, online/distance learning, learning analytics, immersive tech (VR/AR), game-based learning, mobile learning, CS/computational thinking across all education levels. Topic area alone does not redirect a paper between these two. — Confidence: HIGH

## 2. Contribution type & evidence bar

**DIFFERENCE 2a — C&E's defining filter is the "wider education community / beyond one setting" test plus a learning-outcome connection.** C&E's desk screen reportedly turns on: (1) substantive edtech contribution, (2) a learning-outcomes connection (empirical or theoretical), (3) methodological rigor, (4) relevance beyond one course/tool/institution. The classic C&E desk-reject is a well-executed but narrow tool evaluation, or an AI/analytics paper whose outcome is satisfaction/intention-to-use/clicks/time-on-task without a stronger learning/teaching construct.
- Source: https://manusights.com/blog/computers-and-education-submission-guide — Confidence: MEDIUM (secondary analysis, but aligned with official scope wording)

**DIFFERENCE 2b — BJET's bar is broader on contribution type, comparable on quality.** BJET's empirical bar is "significant research studies... of a high scholarly quality" but it does NOT require every paper to be empirical or to demonstrate learning outcomes — a theoretical or methodological contribution can carry a BJET paper on its own. The redirect implication: a strong conceptual/theory/methods paper that C&E would bounce for lacking empirical learning-impact evidence can be in-scope at BJET on its theoretical/methodological merit.
- Source: https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/14678535 ; BJET author guidelines — Confidence: MEDIUM

**DIFFERENCE 2c — Practitioner-facing evidence framing is mandatory at BJET.** Every BJET paper must carry structured **Practitioner Notes** with three headings: "What is already known about this topic," "What this paper adds," "Implications for practice and/or policy," written in language accessible to non-researcher educators. Papers lacking them face rejection/revision. C&E has NO practitioner-notes requirement; its equivalent gate is **Highlights** (3–5 bullets, ≤85 chars each) — a discoverability device, not a practice-translation device.
- Sources: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/14678535/homepage/guide.htm ; https://www.veletsianos.com/2010/12/20/bjet-adds-practitioner-notes/ ; https://manusights.com/blog/computers-and-education-submission-guide (C&E Highlights) — Confidence: HIGH for both requirements
- **Redirect significance:** This is a concrete, mechanical tell. If a draft is framed around practice/policy implications for working educators, BJET's structure rewards it; if it is framed around theory/practice advancement for the research community with no practice-translation layer, C&E fits the framing better. The presence/absence of Practitioner Notes is itself a fingerprint of which journal a paper was written for.

## 3. Method & framing preferences

**DIFFERENCE 3a — Stated method openness.** BJET states plainly it accepts "qualitative, quantitative or mixed methods studies." C&E does not foreground qualitative work to the same degree; its scope and desk-screen emphasize rigor and (esp.) a demonstrable learning-outcomes connection, which in practice tilts the published corpus toward quantitative and design-based/experimental and mixed designs. C&E's official text never enumerates qualitative as a welcomed mode the way BJET does.
- Sources: BJET guidelines (qual/quant/mixed) https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/hub/journal/14678535/forauthors.html ; C&E scope — Confidence: MEDIUM (BJET enumeration HIGH; the "C&E quant tilt" is an inference from the learning-outcomes emphasis, flagged LOW for the tilt magnitude)

**DIFFERENCE 3b — Theory expectations differ in KIND, not just degree.** C&E wants theory in service of demonstrated educational impact ("extends theory and practice" — theory tied to learning/teaching consequences). BJET will accept a "theoretical perspective" as the contribution itself. So: theory-as-lens-for-impact-study → either journal; theory-as-the-deliverable → BJET.
- Sources: both scope statements above — Confidence: MEDIUM

**COULD NOT VERIFY:** A precise published quantitative breakdown (e.g., % qualitative vs quantitative articles) for either journal in a comparable recent window. The qual-vs-quant tilt is asserted from scope wording and editorial-screen descriptions, not from a counted sample. Stated as inference, not fact.

## 4. House writing-style / structural differences

| Feature | C&E | BJET |
|---|---|---|
| Abstract length | ≤250 words, unstructured ("concise and factual") | ~250 words, narrative but with prescribed content beats (aims+context → methods → outcomes → conclusions/implications) |
| Mandatory front-matter device | **Highlights** (3–5 bullets, ≤85 chars) | **Practitioner Notes** (3 fixed headings, practice-facing) |
| Typical research-article length | No tight cap advertised; full-length empirical articles (commonly ~8,000+ words in practice) | Original & Review articles "normally 5,000–6,000 words" excluding refs (special sections allow ~6,000, extensions by request) |
| Article types | Articles; Reviews | Original Articles; Review Articles; Colloquium (short); plus theoretical/methodological pieces |
| Reference style | Elsevier (APA-style, name–date) | APA |
| Video abstracts | Not a standard offering | BJET offers/encourages **video abstracts** |

- Sources: C&E guide for authors (abstract ≤250, Highlights ≤85 chars) https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-and-education/publish/guide-for-authors and https://manusights.com/blog/computers-and-education-submission-guide ; BJET word limits https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/hub/journal/14678535/forauthors.html ; BJET video abstracts https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/hub/journal/14678535/bjet_video_abstracts.htm — Confidence: HIGH for the devices and BJET word limit; MEDIUM for "C&E no tight cap."

**DIFFERENCE 4a — Length is a real discriminator.** BJET's ~5,000–6,000-word norm is materially shorter than the typical full-length C&E empirical article. A long, multi-study, heavily-instrumented empirical paper fits C&E's expectations more comfortably than BJET's word ceiling. A tight, single-study paper fits BJET's length better.
- Confidence: MEDIUM

## 5. Concrete "fits one, not the other" distinctions (the Redirect Test cases)

**Fits C&E, NOT BJET:**
1. A large, multi-study empirical paper with rigorous learning-outcome measurement (e.g., a controlled study showing an AI tutor improves validated conceptual-understanding scores), framed for the research community, ~8,000+ words, with Highlights but no practitioner-facing translation. → C&E's learning-impact bar and length norms reward it; it would strain BJET's 6,000-word limit and lacks the practice-facing framing BJET structures around. (HIGH)
2. A systematic review / meta-analysis with explicit research questions, an analysis framework, PRISMA, and conclusions about learning effects across many settings. → Named in C&E's scope; fits C&E's "broad relevance" mandate squarely. (HIGH — both accept reviews, but C&E's scope explicitly courts meta-analyses with impact framing.)

**Fits BJET, NOT C&E:**
3. A purely **theoretical or conceptual** contribution (a new framework/perspective on edtech) OR a **methodological development** paper (a new analytic method for edtech data) with no empirical learning-outcome demonstration. → BJET's scope explicitly headlines "theoretical perspectives, methodological developments"; C&E's desk screen would likely flag the missing learning-outcomes/impact evidence. (MEDIUM–HIGH)
4. A **practice/implementation-focused** study — an institution-level or LMIC rollout, a longitudinal system-level deployment, or a candid report on an edtech initiative that ran into problems — written for educators/policymakers with strong Practitioner Notes. → On-brand for BJET (implementation research, "failed initiatives," practice framing); at risk at C&E as a "single-setting / delivery-platform / narrow evaluation" unless wider relevance is heavily foregrounded. (MEDIUM–HIGH)
5. A **short work-in-progress or position piece** ("questioning received wisdom"). → Has a home in BJET's Colloquium; no vehicle at C&E. (MEDIUM)

## 6. Honest non-differences / unverifiable points (do NOT use as redirect signals)

- **Topic coverage** (AI, VR, analytics, online learning, game-based, mobile, CS ed): essentially identical menus. Not a discriminator. (HIGH)
- **Overall prestige/selectivity:** Both are top-tier and highly selective (C&E JIF ~11+; BJET ~4% acceptance). Not a clean redirect signal — you don't "step down" from one to the other. (HIGH)
- **Exact qual/quant publication ratio:** asserted as a tilt, not measured. Treat as inference. (LOW)
- **C&E maximum word count:** not advertised as a hard cap; "longer than BJET" is inferred from typical published article length, not a stated rule. (MEDIUM)
